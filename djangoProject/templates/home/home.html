Sustainability Bingo

How does it work?

Sustainability bingo lets you help the environment while having fun! Complete various challenges to tick boxes off of the bingo board and win leaf coins. These coins can be exchanged for seeds in the store, helping you to grow your very own virtual garden. Challenges can range from online quizzes and articles, to exploring campus and scanning QR codes. Learn more about the environment, and how we can help it, while growing your in-game garden! Good luck!

Sustainable Food

One of our main priorities in helping the environment is creating a future where all food originates from sustainable food sources. Currently, the UK imports the majority of its fresh fruit and vegetables from overseas, which is not sustainable for multiple reasons. First, the transport of these goods is a massive source of carbon emissions, further contributing to climate change. Secondly, the countries these goods are imported from often do not produce the goods in a sustainable way. Often, these goods are grown in areas which were originally rainforests, and then sold to nations such as the UK rather than contributing to the local economy. The way to increase food sustainability is to limit the amount of food sold and bought which has been imported from overseas. Another way is to reduce the amount of food waste we produce.

Preventing Climate Change

This is a sustainability goal which has become much more prevalent in recent years. Carbon emissions must be reduced to ensure that the greenhouse effect doesn’t continue to affect the planet. There are many ways to do this, but the most commonly discussed are sustainable energy sources, and reducing energy usage. This can be as small as turning off lights when leaving a room, and as big as introducing energy sources such as solar panels and wind turbines. Many countries are implementing a net-zero carbon emissions plan for the future, with hopes to further decrease the chance and risk of climate change.

Reducing Poverty

Sustainability isn’t all about the environment. It also includes increasing how sustainable peoples lives are economically. Another key goal for a sustainable future is to reduce the number of people who have to live in extreme poverty. This, however, can be extremely challenging, as poverty has been a long-standing problem throughout our history. Despite this, there are still things we can do to reduce it. This can include donating to charities, helping volunteer in local communities, or raising awareness.

Sustainable Cities and Communities

The last main goal of this app is ensuring the sustainability of our cities and urban areas. 57% of the world’s population live in cities, so it’s important to keep these cities liveable and safe for people to live in both the near and far future. A lot of this can link back to sustainable food, water and energy, as cities use much of each of these. Additionally, we need to create green spaces, ensure that buildings can last through natural disasters or extreme weather, and help to grow strong local communities that can support each other through hard times.
